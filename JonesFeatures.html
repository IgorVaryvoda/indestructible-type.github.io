Uncompromising Design

Advanced Kerning Pairs
      In order to maintain consistent letter spacing, Jones* uses many kerning pairs. Some fonts only kern the most obvious pairs, such as “AV”. Others have no kerning whatsoever. Jones* has deataled kering that includes subtle pairings such as “ke” and “ox”. This results in a font with much more refined and durable spacing.

Contextual Substitutions
      Good fonts have letters that look good in every context. A great font adapts to context. When designing the lowercase “f”, a problem arose. When used in words like “found” an “f” that makes a full curve at the top is more rounded and pleasant. However in words like “file” the full curve of the “f” overlaps with the dot of the “i”. Instead of compromising, indestructible type* incorporated both into our designs. When paired with letters such as “a” and “u” the “f” makes a full curve. When paired with letters like “k” or “j” the f automatically adjusts.
