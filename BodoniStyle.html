Bodoni* includes seven styles:

(not including point-size variants)


Bodoni* Book & italic
Book is the ideal weight for setting paragraphs of text. It is designed to look well-composed and balanced at smaller sizes and is kerned to be easy on the eyes over longer sections of text. Although functional and elegant at larger sizes, it is suggested Bodoni* Book be reserved for smaller text sizes.






Bodoni* Medium & italic
Medium is designed to look well-balanced and function better at display sizes. It features slightly tighter kerning and is well suited for headlines and titles. It is not recommended for body text.






Bodoni* Bold & italic
No typeface is complete without a bold weight. Text written in bold is more important than its surrounding text. Bold is to be used like curse-words: sparingly. If it is over-used it loses its meaning.






Bodoni* Fatface
The Fatface was invented and popularized during the industrial revolution. With the rapidly changing times, graphic-designers looked for a new typographic style to cut through the noise. The result was the Fatface. It is as loud, unapologetic and brassy as the nineteen-hundreds America that invented it. It is at home on Victorian posters advertising the circus. indestructible type* celebrates this era with a new weight of Bodoni*: Fatface. Fatface is exclusively for display sizes and does not yet come with a complementary italic.
